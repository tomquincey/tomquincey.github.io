Hooke's Law

Summary

Newton's laws of motion describe how an any object moves when forces act upon on it (as well as giving some insight into the nature of forces themselves).

Newton's 1st Law:
"An object in motion stays in motion"
When no force is acting upon an object, it continues to move at the same speed in the same direction

Newton's 2nd Law:
The acceleration of an object is calculated from the net force acting upon it using \[F = ma\] where \(m\) is the mass of the object

Newton's 3rd Law:
("For every action there is an equal and opposite reaction")

WHAT ? — Lil John

Okay

Of course knowing all this about forces is only so useful if we actually know what a force is. The best explanation of force I have come across is the slightly dopey notion of force simply being how hard you push or pull something.

Newton's 2nd law is only useful if you actually have another law telling you what \(F\) is!

When \(a \ne 0\), there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\): \[x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.\]

Despite being a scary 3-syllabled Latin word, interia is a concept you are very familiar with. It simply means the desire of something to continue doing what it's doing. Familiar examples include the desire to go back to sleep in the morning, the unwillingness of the kids these days (TM) to get off their phones, etc.

Test-Driven Development.

You may have noticed that, when F is zero, then a is zero; no acceleration (i.e. no change in velocity) means velcity stays the same when force is zero, so Newton's 1st law is actually included in the 2nd law!

This turns out to be a consequence of the Law of Conservation of Momentum, which is the most important law of all physics (in my opinion at least).
